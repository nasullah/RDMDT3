  • Inclusion Criteria

    • Generalised (not patchy) scalp hypotrichosis from 6 months of age or earlier with no improvement, AND • Patient older than three years

    Exclusion Criteria

    • Patchy hypotrichosis • Patchy or episodic regrowth • Syndromic congenital hypotrichosis including ectodermal dysplasias, alopecia with vitamin D resistant rickets, hypotrichosis with dysmorphic facies, hypotrichosis with spondyloepimetaphyseal dysplasia

    Prior Genetic Testing

    - Results should have been reviewed for all genetic tests undertaken, including disease-relevant genes in exome sequencing data. The patient is not eligible if they have a molecular diagnosis for their condition. - Genetic testing should continue according to routine local practice for this phenotype regardless of recruitment to the project; results of these tests must be submitted via the ‘Genetic investigations’ section of the data capture tool to allow comparison of WGS with current standard testing. PLEASE NOTE: The sensitivity of WGS compared to current diagnostic genetic testing has not yet been established. It is therefore important that tests which are clinically indicated under local standard practice continue to be carried out.

  • Absent hair

    Alopecia totalis

    Loss of all scalp hair.

    Congenital alopecia totalis

    Loss of all scalp hair with congenital onset.

    Sparse scalp hair

    Decreased number of head hairs per unit area. Comment: Hypotrichosis should not be used as a synonym as, formally, it means underdevelopment of the hair. No normal values for number of hairs per unit area exist.

    Hypotrichosis of the scalp

    Reduced or lacking hair growth of the scalp.

    Progressive hypotrichosis

    Progressively reduced or lacking hair growth.

    Slow-growing scalp hair

    Scalp hair whose growth is slower than normal.

    Abnormal hair quantity

    An abnormal amount of hair.

    Abnormal hair pattern

    An abnormality of the distribution of hair growth.

    Congenital abnormal hair pattern

    A congenital abnormality of the distribution of hair growth.

    Congenital posterior occipital alopecia

    Loss of hair in the occipital region of the scalp with congenital onset.

    Abnormality of the hairline

    The hairline refers to the outline of hair of the head. An abnormality of the hairline can refer to an unusually low or high border between areas of the scalp with and without hair or to abnormal projections of scalp hair.

    Abnormality of the frontal hairline

    An anomaly in the placement or shape of the hairline (trichion) on the forehead, that is, the border between skin on the forehead that has head hair and that does not.

    Abnormality of the posterior hairline

    An anomaly in the placement or shape of the hairline (trichion) on the back of the head (neck), that is, the border between skin on the back of the head that has head hair and that does not.

    Sparse anterior scalp hair

    Decreased number of head hairs per unit area on the anterior region of the scalp.

    Temporal hypotrichosis

    Reduced or lacking hair growth in the temporal region (i.e., around the temples on the side of the skull).

    Absent eyebrow

    Absence of the eyebrow.

    Aplasia/Hypoplasia of the eyebrow

    Absence or underdevelopment of the eyebrow.

    Sparse eyebrow

    Decreased density/number and/or decreased diameter of eyebrow hairs. Comment: Sparseness can be regional (medial, central, lateral) or total. These modifiers should be incorporated into the term, when appropriate.

    Sparse lateral eyebrow

    Decreased density/number and/or decreased diameter of lateral eyebrow hairs. Comment: Abnormal sparseness of the eyebrows laterally.

    Absent eyelashes

    Lack of eyelashes. Comment: Often this finding is congenital and associated with alopecia universalis, but this should be coded separately.

    Sparse eyelashes

    Decreased density/number of eyelashes.

    Abnormality of the eyelashes

    An abnormality of the eyelashes.

    Abnormality of hair texture

    An abnormality of the texture of the hair.

    Brittle scalp hair

    Fragile, easily breakable scalp hair.

    Fine hair

    Hair that is fine or thin to the touch.

    Woolly scalp hair

    Uncombable hair

    Hair that is disorderly, stands out from the scalp, and cannot be combed flat.

    Abnormal hair laboratory examination

    Pili canaliculi

    Uncombable hair.

    Pili torti

    Pili (from Latin pilus, hair) torti (from Latin tortus, twisted) refers to short and brittle hairs that appear flattened and twisted when viewed through a microscope.

    Trichodysplasia

    Developmental dysplasia of the hair.

    Trichorrhexis nodosa

    Trichorrhexis nodosa is the formation of nodes along the hair shaft through which breakage readily occurs. It is thus a focal defect in the hair fiber that is characterized by thickening or weak points (nodes) that cause the hair to break off easily. The result is defective, abnormally fragile hair.

    Alopecia universalis

    Loss of all hair on the entire body.

    Generalized hypotrichosis

    Reduced or lacking hair growth in a generalized distribution.

    Coarse hair

    Hair shafts are rough in texture.

    Erythema

    Redness of the skin, caused by hyperemia of the capillaries in the lower layers of the skin.

    Pruritus

    Pruritus is an itch or a sensation that makes a person want to scratch. This term refers to an abnormally increased disposition to experience pruritus.

    Fair hair

    A lesser degree of hair pigmentation than would otherwise be expected. Comment: For instance, in Angelman syndrome, reduced expression of the gene OCA2 is associated with light-colored hair and fair skin, such that affected children often have noticeably fairer hair than their unaffected relatives.

    Generalized papillary lesions

    Follicular hyperkeratosis

    A skin condition characterized by excessive development of keratin in hair follicles, resulting in rough, cone-shaped, elevated papules resulting from closure of hair follicles with a white plug of sebum.

    Sparse body hair

    Sparseness of the body hair.
