  • Inclusion Criteria

    • Two or more histologically confirmed PJS-type hamartomatous polyps OR
    • Any number of PJS-type polyps detected in one individual who has a confirmed family history of PJS in FDR OR
    • Characteristic mucocutaneous pigmentation in an individual who has a confirmed family history of PJS OR
    • Any number of PJS-type polyps in an individual who also has characteristic mucocutaneous pigmentation.

    Unaffected individuals should not be recruited in this disorder. Recruitment should favour multiplex families over single isolated cases. These singleton recruits will not contribute to the overall singleton monitoring metrics applied to GMCs.

  • Abnormality of skin pigmentation

    An abnormality of the pigmentation of the skin.

    Perioral hyperpigmentation

    Increased pigmentation, either focal or generalized, of the skin surrounding the vermilion of the lips.

    Comment:
    Periorbital hyperpigmentation may be accompanied by Lip freckles, but this should be assessed separately.

    Abnormal pigmentation of the oral mucosa

    An abnormality of the pigmentation of the mucosa of the mouth.

    Large intestinal polyposis

    The presence of multiple polyps in the large intestine.

    Adenomatous colonic polyposis

    Presence of multiple adenomatous polyps in the colon.

    Hamartomatous polyps

    Polyp-like protrusions which are histologically hamartomas. These can occur throughout the gastrointestinal tract.

    Comment:
    Patients with Cowden-Syndrom for example often have multiple hamartomatous gastrointestinal polyps.

    Small intestinal polyposis

    The presence of multiple polyps in the small intestine.

    Intussusception

    An abnormality of the intestine in which part of the intestine invaginates (telescopes) into another part of the intestine.

    Comment:
    Intussusception can lead to intestinal obstruction as well as to the interruption of the blood supply to the intestine. It can affect small or large bowel.

    Intestinal bleeding

    Bleeding from the intestines.

    Cervix cancer

    A tumor of the uterine cervix.

    Testicular neoplasm

    The presence of a neoplasm of the testis.

    Colon cancer

    Breast carcinoma

    The presence of a carcinoma of the breast.

    Neoplasm of the small intestine

    The presence of a neoplasm of the small intestine.

    Ovarian neoplasm

    A tumor (abnormal growth of tissue) of the ovary.
