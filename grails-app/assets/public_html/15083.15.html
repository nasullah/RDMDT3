  • Inclusion Criteria

    - All six of the following: - Photosensitivity - Papulovesicular eruption - Typical varioliform scarring - Significantly elevated Epstein-Barr viral load in the blood - Positive phototest responses to UVA - clinical diagnosis must be made by an experienced photodermatologist able to differentiate this from other photodermatoses clinically

    Exclusion Criteria

    - No evidence of Epstein-Barr viral infection

    Prior Genetic Testing

    - Results should have been reviewed for all genetic tests undertaken, including disease-relevant genes in exome sequencing data. The patient is not eligible if they have a molecular diagnosis for their condition. - Genetic testing should continue according to routine local practice for this phenotype regardless of recruitment to the project; results of these tests must be submitted via the ‘Genetic investigations’ section of the data capture tool to allow comparison of WGS with current standard testing. PLEASE NOTE: The sensitivity of WGS compared to current diagnostic genetic testing has not yet been established. It is therefore important that tests which are clinically indicated under local standard practice continue to be carried out.

  • Cutaneous photosensitivity

    An increased sensitivity of the skin to light. Photosensitivity may result in a rash upon exposure to the sun (which is known as photodermatosis). Photosensitivity can be diagnosed by phototests in which light is shone on small areas of skin.

    Lymphadenopathy

    Enlargment (swelling) of a lymph node.

    Lymphoma

    A cancer originating in lymphocytes and presenting as a solid tumor of lymhpoid cells.

    Photophobia

    Excessive sensitivity to light with the sensation of discomfort or pain in the eyes due to exposure to bright light. Comment: Note that the term photodysphoria is used to describe extreme photophobia.

    Chemosis

    Edema (swelling) of the bulbar conjunctiva.

    Keratoconjunctivitis

    Inflammation of the cornea and conjunctiva.

    Corneal ulceration

    Disruption of the epithelial layer of the cornea with involvement of the underlying stroma.

    Iritis

    Inflammation of the iris.

    Uveitis

    Inflammation of one or all portions of the uveal tract. Comment: The uvea is the pigmented middle layer of the eye consisting of the iris and ciliary body together with the choroid.

    Episcleritis

    Inflammation of the episclera, a thin layer of tissue covering the white part (sclera) of the eye.

    Recurrent aphthous stomatitis

    Recurrent episodes of ulceration of the oral mucosa. Comment: Recurrent aphthous stomatitis can be classified into three forms, with minor ulcers (over 80% of RAS cases) being less than 1 cm in diameter and self-limiting; major ulcers being over 1 cm in diameter with potential scar formation; and herpetiform ulcers manifesting as recurrent episodes with multiple small ulcers throughout the oral mucosa.

    Fever

    Elevated body temperature due to failed thermoregulation. Comment: Hyperthermia is defined as a temperature greater than 37.5-38.3 ??C (100-101 ??F).

    Weight loss

    Reduction inexisting body weight.

    Hepatomegaly

    Abnormally increased size of the liver.

    Splenomegaly

    Abnormal increased size of the spleen.

    Elevated hepatic transaminases

    Elevations of the levels of SGOT and SGPT in the serum. SGOT (serum glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase) and SGPT (serum glutamic pyruvic transaminase) are transaminases primarily found in the liver and heart and are released into the bloodstream as the result of liver or heart damage. SGOT and SGPT are used clinically mainly as markers of liver damage.

    Leukopenia

    An abnormal decreased number of leukocytes in the blood.

    Thrombocytopenia

    A reduction in the number of circulating thrombocytes. Comment: Thrombocytopenia can be divided into three major causes; 1) low production of platelets in the bone marrow; 2) intravascular breakdown of thrombocytes; and 3) increased turnover of platelets in spleen or liver (extravascular).
